🇿🇲 Why This Matters in Zambia

Zambia has one of the highest adolescent pregnancy rates in Africa. Nearly 3 in 10 girls become pregnant before age 18, with rates as high as 37% in rural areas. Early pregnancy often means dropping out of school, limiting future opportunities, and facing serious health risks.

Additionally, while 83% of women have been tested for HIV, only 42% of young women and 43% of young men have comprehensive knowledge of HIV prevention. Knowledge is power — and this site is here to give you that power.

Understanding Your Body

🌸 For Girls

  • Menstruation: A normal monthly cycle. Periods usually start between ages 10-15.
  • Breast development: Usually the first sign of puberty.
  • Hips widen: Your body prepares for potential future childbirth.
  • Mood changes: Hormones can make emotions feel stronger. This is normal!
  • Discharge: Clear or white discharge is normal. If it smells bad or itches, see a nurse.

🌿 For Boys

  • Voice changes: Your voice may "crack" as it deepens.
  • Facial/body hair: Hair grows on face, chest, underarms, and pubic area.
  • Muscles develop: Shoulders broaden and muscles grow.
  • Wet dreams: Ejaculation during sleep is completely normal.
  • Erections: Happen often and sometimes at embarrassing times. Totally normal!

🛡️ HIV Prevention — The ABCD Method

🚫
A — Abstain

The only 100% effective way to prevent HIV and pregnancy.

💑
B — Be Faithful

If sexually active, stick to one uninfected partner.

🎈
C — Condomize

Use condoms correctly, every single time.

💊
D — Drugs (ARVs/PrEP)

Take ARVs if HIV+ or PrEP if at high risk.

⚠️ When to See a Health Worker

  • Missed period (if sexually active) — check for pregnancy
  • Burning pain when urinating — could be an STI
  • Unusual discharge, sores, or warts around genitals
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • You think you may have been exposed to HIV — get tested immediately (PEP works within 72 hours)
